1

George Washington didn't really hack down a cherry tree.

You may have heard the tale of America's first president harming his dad's cherry tree with an ax as a little fellow. At the point when defied about it, he purportedly admitted and stated, "I can't lie." While it's a commendable tale about trustworthiness, this is really a fantasy made up by one of George Washington's biographers, Mason Locke Weems.

2

Cherry trees have a short life expectancy.

Regularly, they just last around 16-20 years. However, certain species have an any longer future. Dark cherry trees, for instance, can satisfy 250 years.

3

You can get arrested for severing a bloom.

Yep, think again before you decide to pluck one of these pink beauties. Removing a blossom is viewed as vandalism of federal property in Washington, D.C., which can lead to a citation or even arrest.
